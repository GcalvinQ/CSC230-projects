The Unviversity of Tampa's Maker's Space

The Makerspace on the 6th floor of UT's Technology Building provides Computer Science (CS) majors and minors with resources for rapid prototyping. It's a place where CS students can design, fabricate, assemble, and test their ideas related to projects in the Computer Science curriculum. It supports various phases of project development from design, prototyping, and testing to fabrication, assembly, and production. The Makerspace is currently only available to CS majors and minors.

Equipment

3D Printer

Two Makerbot Replicator+ single extruder 3D printers support fabrication of designs up to 11.6” by 7.6” by 6.5” high with PLA, tough PLA, and other filaments. Using fused filament fabrication technology, they feature a flexible grip surface (non-heated bed) and 100 micron layer resolution. These 3D printers are great for rough 3D prototyping and are accessed through desktop computers in the Makerspace.

One Ultimaker S3 dual extruder 3D printer supports fabrication of designs up to 9” by 7.4” by 7.9” high with PLA, tough PLA, Nylon, ABS, CPE, and other filaments. Using fused filament fabrication technology, it features a heated glass surface and 20 to 600 micron layer resolution. This 3D printer is great for polished designs and is accessed via USB.
Laser

Two Glowforge Pro desktop lasers enable cutting and engraving of wood, acrylic, glass, fabric, cardboard, leather, and some metals. They accommodate stock that is 20” wide by 12” long or greater, at a maximum height of 2”. The lasers can be dangerous and can only be accessed through a Makerspace worker.
Milling Machine

The Bantam Tools Desktop CNC milling machine allows milling of wood, many metals (but is optimized for aluminum), FR-1 copper-clad PCB (printed circuit board) blanks, and more. It supports designs up to 7” by 9” by 3.3” high (208 in3). It can be used to fabricate custom PCBs. The CNC milling machine can be dangerous and can only be accessed through a Makerspace worker.
Soldering Iron

Three soldering stations allow students to populate custom milled printed circuit boards with electronic components.
Function Generator

A function generator is a piece of electronic test instrument used to generate and deliver standard waveforms, typically sine and square waves, to a device under test. It can be used to test a design or confirm that a piece of electronic equipment is working as intended.
Multimeter

The multimeter is used for measuring of voltage of direct current and alternating current, measuring of direct and alternatig current and measuring of resistance, electrical capacitance and inductance.
Oscilloscope

The oscilloscope can test and display voltage signals as waveforms, visual representations of the variation of voltage over time. The signals are plotted on a graph, which shows how the signal changes. The vertical (Y) access represents the voltage measurement and the horizontal (X) axis represents time.
Power Supply

The power supply creates and supplies a stable Direct Current (DC) from the AC power supply of the outlet and can also be a power source to run electronic circuits or to check electronic devices.
Vacuum Former

The Formech Compac Mini desktop vacuum former quickly shapes HIPS, ABS, PETG, and other plastic sheets in the form of an existing 3D design. It has a forming area of 11” by 9” and has a maximum draw depth of 5.5”.
Roland Vinyl Cutter

The Roland CAMM-1 GS-24 desktop cutter custom cuts vinyl, heat transfer material, and more, from 2” to 27.5” wide (but also supports oversized designs using a novel tiling function).
Power Press

The PowerPress heat press can heat seal designs up to 15” by 15” onto fabrics (natural or synthetic), ceramic, some plastics, and more.
HP DesignJet

Four HP EliteOne 800 G6 all-in-one desktop PCs allow students to create designs and connect to various devices that take their ideas from concept to reality.
